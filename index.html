Who is Imran Khan?

Former Prime Manister of Pakistan

Imran Khan (born October 5, 1952, Lahore, Pakistan) is an antiestablishment politician in Pakistan who in 2022 became the first prime minister (2018–22) to be removed by a parliamentary vote. He rose to fame as a cricket player who led Pakistan’s national team to a Cricket World Cup victory in 1992.

He later entered politics as a critic of government corruption in Pakistan, although he faced corruption charges of his own in 2022 after falling out with the politically powerful army.

Achievements

Education, Cricket, Hospital,and Politics

Keble_College_Oxford

Imran Khan attended the University of Oxford in England, where he studied Philosophy, Politics, and Economics (PPE) at Keble College. He completed his undergraduate degree in 1975. During his time at Oxford, Imran Khan was also involved in cricket and played for the university's cricket team. His education at Oxford played a significant role in shaping his worldview and preparing him for his future endeavors in cricket, philanthropy, and politics.

Cricket Career

Imran Khan was one of the greatest cricketers in the history of Pakistan. He led the Pakistani cricket team to its first-ever World Cup victory in 1992.

Shaukat-Khanum-Memorial-Hospital

Imran Khan founded the First Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital & Research Centre in Lahore, Pakistan, in memory of his mother who died of cancer and second one in Peshawar KPK province. The hospital provides free treatment to cancer patients and is considered one of the most reputable cancer hospitals in the country.

Under Construction Projects

Imran Khan has initiated the development of cancer hospitals in several cities in Pakistan and these upcoming hospitals will be in Karachi, Sindh province, and Quetta, Balochistan province.

The Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI)

The Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI), which translates to Pakistan Movement for Justice, is a political party in Pakistan founded by Imran Khan in 1996. PTI emerged as a major player in Pakistani politics, advocating for anti-corruption, social justice, and welfare programs. Imran Khan's charismatic leadership helped the party gain popularity, especially among young voters.

PTI's Main Ideology

PTI's main ideology revolves around creating a welfare state, promoting education, and improving healthcare. After years of political struggle, PTI won the 2018 general elections, and Imran Khan became the Prime Minister of Pakistan, marking a significant milestone in the party's journey.
